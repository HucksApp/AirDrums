Basis

AirDrum uses accelerometer data to turn your phone into a drumstick, allowing you to play air drums that actually produce sounds. AirDrum is the creation of Eddie Zaneski, Sushanth Kodali, and Ian Lozinski. The initial idea was born at HackNY '13 and was created in under 24 hours. Since then, the app has improved and the code is much cleaner.

A brief history...

We wanted to learn while making something cool and "useful" at the same time. Having recently discovered Google Chrome Super-Sync Sports, which lets users use their mobile device as a game controller, we wanted to utilize the power of websockets. We also wanted to experiment with hardware so we chose accelerometers to measure changes in velocity and the direction the phone was facing. As it turns out, HTML5 has direct access to devices' accelerometer data, so instead of making a native iOS or Android app, it made sense to make a webapp which would scale to multiple devices.

AirDrum was made using the following:
  • node.js
  • express
  • Socket.IO
  • JavaScript AudioFX
  • jQuery mobile
